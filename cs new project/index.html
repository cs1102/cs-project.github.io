Our experience with different Chinese input methods

Chinese is one of the most spoken languages worldwide, and a Chinese input method is a crucial tool for anyone who needs to type in Chinese, whether for work, study, or personal communication. However, unlike English and many European languages, Chinese uses a logographic writing system, which cannot be adapted directly to the keyboard using the Latin alphabet as default.




(A scene in Simpson showing how complicated Chinese characters are and being satire on how to type Chinese using a typewriter)


In this project, we will briefly discuss and share our experience on how different Chinese input methods work, including Stroke count, Pinyin, Voice, and Cangjie input methods.
